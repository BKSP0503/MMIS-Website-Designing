Remote Sensing

1. It is the process of collecting information about an object without being directly in physical contact with the object.
2. It is the process of collecting information about Earth’s surface.
3. Sensors are used to detect, analyze, process and quantify the physical characteristics of the Earth’s surface (Specific area of interest) by the means of measuring emitted or reflected radiation.
4. The output of remote sensing is raster in nature.


Process of Remote sensing


Types of Remote Sensing

It is of 2 types: Active and Passive

Active sensing:

1. In this case energy irradiated from the sun is used as source.
2. Solar energy is reflected or absorbed and is then reemitted.
3. Passive sensors are used to measure the energy.
4. This method can only be used when natural energy is available.
5. E.g.: Landsat, ASTER... etc.

Passive Sensing:

1.In this type of sensing, the sensor is allowed emit radiation towards the target to be investigated.
2. The radiation reflected back from the target is detected and is measured by the sensor.
3. This method is used to obtain information at any time.
4. Large amount of energy is used to adequately illuminate the target.
5. E.g.: LIDAR, RADAR.


Remote Sensing Platforms

Remote sensors are mounted on the following platforms

Ground based platform:

1. Used to record detailed information about the object and the features of the Earth’s surface.
2. Portable handheld photographic cameras andspectroradiometers are most frequently used.

Air-borne platform:

1. Used to record detailed information about the objectvand the features of the Earth’s surface from a particular altitude above the surface to cover more range and obtain more accurate information.
2. Balloons, drones, aircrafts and rockets are used based on the requirement of the intricacy of the details of the information to be recorded.

Space-borne platform:

1. Artificial satellites, space shuttles are used to sense the Earth’s surface with the highest degree of information from space.



Applications of Remote Sensing


GIS

1. A geographic information system (GIS) is a type of database containing geographic data (that is, descriptions of phenomena for which location is relevant), combined with software tools for managing, analyzing and visualizing those data.
2. GIS is integrated with the existing systems to take decisions on the fly with utmost accuracy.
3. GIS is prepared with a reference point and reference date, i.e.; the map is represented with all the points with respect to the location at that date.
4. The output of GIS is vector in nature.


Relationship Between Remote sensing and GIS

1. Remotely sensing a vector map with a geo-reference in context is called remote sensing.
2. Remotely sensing the “area of interest” on the ground and updating the base map with obtained output with respect to thenatural resources is the relationship between RS and GIS.
